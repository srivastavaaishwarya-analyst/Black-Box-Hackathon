# Black-Box-Hackathon

# 🧠 BlackBox Hackathon – AI Human Response Prediction

**Team:** Data Gamblers
---
## 📌 Overview

This project was developed during a 24-hour hackathon focused on a challenging question: *Can AI predict how a human would respond to questions they have never seen before?*

We explored the foundations of AI, personalization, and behavioral modeling by building a system that attempts to simulate human decision-making using survey and personality data.
---
## ❓ Problem Statement

Given a person’s past survey responses, personality traits, and behavioral data, the goal was to design a model that can accurately predict their answers to new, unseen questions.
---
## 📊 Dataset

The dataset included information on 200+ individuals across multiple formats:

* **Personality metrics** (Big Five, cognition, etc.)
* **Behavioral data** (economic games, risk preferences)
* **Survey responses** across multiple domains
* Data provided in **CSV, JSON, and text formats**
---
## ⚙️ Approach

Our team explored different ways to model human responses:

* Performed **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** to understand the dataset
* Selected meaningful features from survey and personality data
* Built initial predictive models to estimate responses
* Iterated on approaches within a limited 24-hour timeframe

**Tools & Technologies:**

* Python
* Pandas / NumPy
* Scikit-learn (or other ML tools if used)
* Google Colab
---
## 🧪 Results

* The model was able to capture general behavioral patterns across individuals
* Performance varied depending on the type of question (objective vs subjective)
* Highlighted the difficulty of predicting nuanced human decisions
---
## 💡 Key Learnings

* Real-world data is complex and requires thoughtful preprocessing
* Modeling human behavior is significantly harder than traditional ML tasks
* Rapid prototyping is essential in time-constrained environments
* Collaboration and teamwork are critical for success in hackathons
